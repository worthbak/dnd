During this session (which Pat and Geoff missed), Zerai, Shelf, and Ulrik met back up with Harshnaag and solved a puzzle to gain access to an ancient, massive map room. There they met a Seraph, called The Watcher, and Kevin's new character, Dafis, an elven rogue and member of the Order of the Sixth Watch, who was sent to the Eye of the Allfather to learn about the disappearance of a magical artifact, a conch, from the material plane. 

Together with Dafis, the party learned that the conch the party stole from the Hill Giant Guh and gave to Asmodeus is one of six ancient artifacts, entrusted to giants, that together form the basis of a cosmic prison that holds a primordial, reality-devouring “anti-matter” being (The Watcher referred to it as the Hunger or the Unbound).

Each giant type has one artifact:
- Hill: Conch – connected all artifacts together, and let the Seraph monitor the other artifacts as well as the seal.  
- Fire: Crown – powerful, weapon-like containment power source; dangerous if misused.  
- Frost: Tear – preserves and stabilizes the seal.  
- Stone: Geode – occupies the being’s “mind,” keeping it idle.  
- Cloud: Mirror – controls paths in and out of the prison.
- Storm: Trident – main power source; if corrupted, the prison can break.  

Giants were made custodians of these artifacts, and the Ordning was partly a magical enforcement mechanism to keep them aligned with protecting these artifacts. However, over time, they forgot the true purpose and treated them as status/power items, and when for reasons unknown Annam shattered the Ordning, there was no longer any magical consequence to the misuse of their artifacts. King Heketon, knowing that his power over the various giant factions was no longer ordained, and was merely political, appears to have gone into hiding in order to protect his artifact, the Trident. 

When the party gave the conch to Asmodeus, it left the material plane, and the Watcher could no longer monitor the status and location of the other artifacts. Additionally, Asmodeus didn't take the conch without reason - his plans are unclear, but he likely wishes to gain leverage over other powerful entities. With the status of these sealing artifacts unknown and unprotected, the prison containing this elemental, primordial evil is terrifyingly vulnerable, and the Watcher asked the party to do what they could to secure the remaining five artifacts, and to protect the material plane from certain demise. 

The party weighed several courses of action and next steps, ultimately deciding that disrupting the Fire Giant Duke Zalto's plan to use his artifact, the Crown, to power the Vonindod (which the Watcher warned could have destabilizing consequences for the seal, representing the most pressing threat) was their immediate plan, traveling to Ironslag on their way west to find King Heketon. The party’s main idea is to acquire a fake a power source/artifact for the Vonindod (likely a forged conch or similar) and have a wizard enchant it so it looks and feels real. They’d then trick Duke Zalto into using the fake instead of his crown, ideally preserving the crown’s role in the prison seal. Ideally, they want the fake artifact imbued with teleportation magic, so that when Zalto activates it, it teleports Zalto and/or the Vonindod away or otherwise disables his plan. 

Departing from the Watcher's domain (and after some terrifying discussion of the dreaded *Lasagna Dimension*), the Harshnaag parted with the party, insisting that he needed to strategize with his allies in Waterdeep. He left a limited-use long-range sending stone (one message/day) with Zerai, and lit an enchanted match that teleported him elsewhere. Ulrik also said his goodbyes, noting that he felt a deep longing to explore a life amongst other dwarves in Mirabar, but that he would always be available to the party if they needed him. He left his Cloak of Protection to Dafis, and flew away. 

Exiting the temple, the party was greeted by the sudden appearance of a lesser devil, named Valaxis, who beckoned the party to join him on a journey to Silverymoon, where Asmodeus requested their presence for a meeting at the Margaster estate. All were wary of returning to the estate, but decided that delaying a meeting with Asmodeus, or splitting the party, brought its own perils. The party climbed into Valaxis' nightmare-driven carriage and rocketed into the sky.
