# Session 26 - The Adamantine Dagger and the Descent

*Sat, 25 Apr 26*

### Session Recap

- Picked up after Session 25: Yartar via Harper circle, headed to Triboar to find Orthovier, with Thornhold as the working theory for where Xamlyn fled

### Yartar — Night at the Flowing Pint

- Met Varg at the Yartar Harper safehouse; Dafis got 2 healing potions
- Hired on as paid security for Clem Redwhisker, a halfling merchant running goods to Triboar the next morning
- Tavern night at the Flowing Pint:
  - Shelf busked Baldur's-Gate-top-40 hits and turned the room into a jam — 24 gp in tips
  - Norble disguise-selfed and drank with an invisible Zerai in the corner (Cookie Monster voice now canon)
  - Dafis pickpocketed a drunk dwarf-orc couple (22 gp) and a half-elf Yartar magistrate (21 gp + correspondence)
  - Roy got blackout drunk; barely made the caravan
- Magistrate's documents: Silverymoon fire is rippling across the Coast, Margaster infernal ties now public, Xamlyn's whereabouts unknown to civic authorities

### Triboar — Orthovier Margaster

- Day's ride to Triboar with Clem, no incidents (party forgot to negotiate payment, got nothing)
- Found Orthovier at his leather shop and returned the adamantine dagger from Session 2
- Dafis (Nat 20 Arcana): the dagger is connected to the *power* the Seal contains — a "mirror" of the Hunger
- Orthovier confirmed he is a Margaster — left the family because of their slaving and devil-dealing; not the first to flee (his uncle was killed for it)
- Wanted the dagger back to destroy it. Ancestors were Sword Coast barbarians; they called the bound evil "Gruffsh" — barbarian for *screaming*

### Foehammer's Forge — Destroying the Dagger

- Walked it to Gelrin Foehammer's shop (Session 1 blacksmith, partly rebuilt after the giant attack)
- Orthovier plunged the dagger into molten slag; it resisted and heated red
- Shelf saw a vision in the blade — a massive bloodshot eye on an inky-black creature reached for his mind, trying to compel him to stab Orthovier and seize the dagger. Made the DC 18 Cha save with a 28
- Pushing past the compulsion, Shelf saw the creature's *intent*: a vision of a massive anti-matter being descending on Baldur's Gate, deleting the city brick by brick. This is the Hunger / the Unbound the Watcher described
- Dagger destroyed in a shockwave of purple smoke. Party kept the **hilt** — non-magical, but a "forensic mage" might trace its lineage

### Orthovier's Intel on Xamlyn

- **Thornhold** is the destination — Xamlyn has wanted the family's ancestral fortress back for a century; given to the **Forgeright** dwarven clan when the Margasters were ousted from Waterdeep
- **Drow correspondence:** Xamlyn has been corresponding with Drow from Menzobarranzan — the rumored Underdark passage is real
- **Family makeup:** Xamlyn = mastermind, "all her wood behind one arrow"; Brennan = bodyguard brother; the niece is a 15-year-old powerful mage but won't split off. They move as a unit
- Orthovier taught Shelf the **Thornhold teleportation sigil** (deep in the fortress; Margasters have it too)

### Waterdeep — Harper Drop and the High Road North

- Decided not to use the Thornhold sigil cold (likely ambush). Burned Shelf's 5th-level slot to teleport from Triboar to **Waterdeep** (Harper sigil) instead
- Landed in a damp basement under a Harper fishmonger front; met **Meiril**, the Waterdeep night-shift handler

**What Meiril told us:**
- Smoke seen rising from Thornhold **two days ago**; Forgeright banner pulled down
- Two undercover Harpers — **Joffrey and Nebrontis** — sent to investigate a day ago; no word back
- High road north = 1 day, exposed. Marsh route = 4 days, patrolled by some magical bandit charging tolls (filed for return trip)

- Hired wagon, horses, and Dafis disguised himself as *William* the wagon-loving stableman who refused to be parted from it
- Loaded with empty barrels for cover; rode through the night

### Thornhold

- Forgeright banner has been re-raised — they retook the position
- Played merchants at the checkpoint; cover collapsed instantly, but "Margaster" was the magic word and the guards lit up
- Disarmed and escorted in; William stayed with the wagon

**Thora Stoneshaft (Forgeright captain):**
- Margasters teleported in two days ago — 12-hour occupation. ~15-20 dwarves on duty: 10 killed, 5 taken prisoner
- Margaster force: 3 humans, 2 cambions (gravely wounded but escaped), 2 unidentified fighters
- Thora is coming with us to recover the prisoners and spill Margaster blood

**Hildeg the Survivor:**
- One eye, conscious through the occupation. No drow physically present, but Margasters were heard naming **Yinrith** and **Vorsylla**

**Investigation findings:**
- Hidden teleportation circle behind a false wall in the servants' quarters, accessed via the lower dungeon
- The Margasters were rushed and dropped:
  - **A hand-drawn Underdark map** marking their route from below Thornhold — paths to avoid, mushroom groves, leading somewhere specific
  - **A torn page of dwarven history** referencing **Maergera**, a fire primordial whose heat dwarves have used for eons to forge the purest adamantine

**Theory:**
- Zalto needs some Vonindod power source he can't get from the Zhentarim anymore
- Maergera fits — a captive fire primordial gives Zalto what he needs without him having to use the Ember Crown
- The hard part isn't *finding* a primordial — it's *transporting* one. The drow can move it through the Underdark
- This is likely what Xamlyn is brokering. Cut this off and Zalto stays stuck

### The Descent

- Stone disc on chains, several hundred yards down through rock to the Underdark floor
- Torch strapped to Roy (no darkvision); Dafis went invisible
- Thora descended with party as a Forgeright ally
- Landed in a massive cavern: bioluminescent mushrooms, stalagmites, oppressive dark beyond Zerai's 120ft

### Cliffhanger

- Half a day to a day behind Xamlyn somewhere ahead of us: Drow Yinrith and Forilla, five captive dwarves, two wounded cambions, a fire primordial deal in progress
- Open option: third-party drow vs. duergar if the politics line up

### Key Questions Going Forward

- Is Maergera the actual offer, or is the drow deal about something else?
- Can we intercept before the deal closes?
- What are the drow getting — payment, slaves, an artifact?
- Are Joffre and Nebrontis alive down here?
